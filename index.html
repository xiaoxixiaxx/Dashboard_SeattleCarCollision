Little Backstory

This project is to visually display the spatial location of where collisions occurred in the Seattle region. The map would display the individual and the cluster of the incidents. While the plots display the aggregated count of each month.

This project is inspired by The Rothenberg Law Firm article (LINK) that discusses how COVID-19 influence on car accidents (written on Oct 26, 2020). Interestingly, they stated that during COVID-19 have the highest deadly rate of car crashes than the previous year (by 2 - 11%, various on each month and states). Even though that the amount of incidents is less than the previous year. In another word, during COVID-19, fewer drivers are going out on the road, which could lead to more psychological behavior (and other factors such as economic) of driving on empty/ less busy roads. This research article from Accident Analysis & Prevention can help explains in-depth (LINK) .

We were planning to show the statistics rate of car collisions, and comparsion to the overall USA. Unfortunately, we don't have time. Maybe in the future.
